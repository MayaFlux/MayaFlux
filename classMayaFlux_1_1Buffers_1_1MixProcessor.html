Detailed Description

Processes multiple audio buffers and mixes their data into a single output buffer.

This processor allows multiple audio sources to be registered, each with its own mix level. It can handle both continuous mixing and one-time mixes based on the once flag.

This is the primary mechanism for single audio buffer to be mixed into multiple channels. Compared to Nodes, buffers are inherently single channel due to their transient nature and the architecture of MayaFlux that adds processors to buffers instead of processing buffers themselves Hence, process once and supply to multiple channels is the most efficient method to send concurrent data to multiple channels.
